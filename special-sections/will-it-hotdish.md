## The Final Verdict

**Everything can be a hotdish.** This is the fundamental theorem of Midwestern cooking. Give us any cuisine on earth, a can of cream-of-something, a crunchy topping, and a 9x13 pan, and we will hotdish it.

Is it always a good idea? No.  
Will we do it anyway? Absolutely.  
Will we bring it to the potluck? Only if someone asks what we're bringing, at which point we'll say "Oh, just a little something" and produce a 9x13 pan of Pad Thai Hotdish like it's perfectly normal.

Because in the Midwest, the hotdish is not just a dish.  
It's a philosophy.
