2. **Cook the sugar.** In a large heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the sugar, corn syrup, water, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture begins to boil.

3. **Add the peanuts.** Once boiling, add the raw peanuts. Attach your candy thermometer and continue cooking, stirring frequently, until the mixture reaches 300Â°F (hard-crack stage). ## Tips & Variations

- **Candy Thermometer is Non-Negotiable.** The difference between 290Â°F (chewy and underwhelming) and 310Â°F (burnt and bitter) is narrow. Use a thermometer. Clip it to the pot. Trust the numbers.
- **Raw Peanuts:** Use raw (not roasted) peanuts â€” they'll roast in the sugar syrup as it cooks. Pre-roasted peanuts will over-toast and taste bitter by the time the sugar reaches 300Â°F.
- **Baking Soda Magic:** The baking soda reacts with the acidic sugar syrup, creating thousands of tiny CO2 bubbles that make the brittle airy and light. Without it, you'd have peanut toffee â€” good but not brittle. Don't skip it.
- **Cashew Brittle:** Substitute cashews for peanuts. Add them later (at 275Â°F) since cashews are softer and burn more easily.
- **Spiced Brittle:** Add Â¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper with the baking soda for a sweet-heat brittle that's dangerously addictive.
- **Microwave Method:** For beginners, there are microwave peanut brittle recipes that work surprisingly well. They won't have quite the same depth of caramelization, but they're much less intimidating.
- **Humidity Warning:** Peanut brittle absorbs moisture from the air. Don't make it on a rainy or humid day, and store it sealed. Soggy brittle is sad brittle.
