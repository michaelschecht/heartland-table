## Chosen Direction: Modern Rustic Farmhouse (Hybrid with Heritage Accents)

Blends classic farmhouse elements with clean, modern design principles. Fresh, inviting, elevated Midwest charm ‚Äî with Heritage touches for special sections (Grandma's Secrets, Church Basement Classics).

---

## 1. Visual Identity

### Overall Aesthetic
- **Style:** Modern Rustic Farmhouse with Heritage Accents
- **Mood:** Warm, inviting, authentic, approachable, with curated nostalgia
- **Feeling:** Like a cherished family heirloom, yet fresh and relevant
- **Emotional Response:** Comfort, discovery, connection to heartland traditions

### Color Palette

**Primary (Backgrounds & Canvas):**
- Creamy whites / soft off-whites
- Muted oatmeal and linen tones
- Light greys

**Accent Colors:**
- Soft sage green / muted forest green
- Dusty blue / sky blue
- Warm terracotta / harvest gold / amber
- Deep barn red / cranberry / berry
- Mustard yellow (sparingly)

**Reasoning:** Organic, comforting, reflects agricultural bounty and Midwest landscapes

---

## 2. Typography

### Display Type (Chapter Titles, H1)
- **Primary:** Playfair Display Bold or Lora
- **Character:** Sophisticated, slightly condensed serif with heritage feel
- **Usage:** Chapter openers, main headings

### Recipe Titles (H2)
- **Font:** Montserrat Medium/Bold or Open Sans Semibold
- **Character:** Clean, modern sans-serif
- **Usage:** Recipe names, section headers

### Body Text (Ingredients, Instructions)
- **Font:** Merriweather or Source Serif Pro
- **Character:** Highly readable, comfortable serif
- **Usage:** Recipe content, cultural notes

### Accent Script (Special Callouts)
- **Font:** Pacifico or Great Vibes (used sparingly)
- **Usage:** "Grandma's Secret" sidebars only

### Hierarchy Guidelines
- Clear size/weight differentiation
- Generous line spacing (1.4-1.6 for body)
- Strong visual hierarchy guides readers effortlessly

---

## 3. Photography Direction

### Style Philosophy: "Authentic Aspirational"
Food should look delicious AND achievable ‚Äî not overly fussy

### Lighting
- **Primary:** Soft, natural golden window light
- **Direction:** Side or back lighting for depth and warmth
- **Feel:** Diffused, gentle, inviting ‚Äî like a sunny kitchen

### Composition
- Mix of overhead flat lays and eye-level shots
- Shallow depth of field for intimacy
- Ample negative space around food
- Focus on texture and steam

### Styling
- **Props:** Cast iron, wooden cutting boards, ceramic plates, vintage glassware, linen napkins, mason jars, wheat stalks
- **Philosophy:** "Perfectly imperfect" ‚Äî crumbs, drips, rumpled napkins add authenticity
- **Portions:** Generous, abundant, hearty

### Process & Environmental Shots
- Key process shots for complex recipes
- Ingredient still lifes as chapter dividers
- Subtle human elements (hands mixing dough)
- Backgrounds: weathered wood, rustic countertops, simple backdrops

## 5. Print Production

### Book Dimensions
- **Recommended Size:** 8.5" √ó 10" or 9" √ó 11" (vertical)
- **Rationale:** Large enough for beautiful photos, manageable in kitchen

### Page Count Estimate
- 90 recipes √ó 2 pages average = 180 pages
- Front matter (intro, TOC, etc.) = 10-15 pages
- Chapter dividers (10 categories) = 10-20 pages
- Special sections = 15-20 pages
- Back matter (index, acknowledgments) = 10-15 pages
- **Total Estimate:** 220-250 pages

### Paper Stock
- **Interior:** Uncoated or matte-coated, 80-100 lb text weight
- **Color:** Slightly off-white/cream for warmth
- **Benefits:** Reduces glare in kitchen, tactile feel

### Cover
- **Type:** Hardcover (casebound)
- **Finish:** Matte laminate (soft touch, fingerprint resistant)
- **Weight:** 100-120 lb cover stock

### Special Finishes
- **Debossing:** Title text for premium texture
- **Spot UV:** Selective gloss on title/elements (optional)
- **Foil Stamping:** Muted gold/copper for title (optional)
- **Ribbon Bookmark:** Accent color from palette

### Binding
- **Type:** Smyth-sewn (critical for lay-flat)
- **Endpapers:** Solid muted color (sage, amber) or subtle pattern

## 6. Printing Options

### Print-on-Demand (Small Runs: 1-100 copies)
- **Providers:** Blurb, Lulu, BookBaby, Amazon KDP
- **Pros:** No upfront inventory, on-demand fulfillment
- **Cons:** Higher per-unit cost ($25-45/book), limited finishes
- **Best For:** Proofs, gifts, limited editions

### Short-Run Offset (100-500 copies)
- **Providers:** Mixam, PrintNinja, 48 Hour Books
- **Pros:** Better quality, more finish options, lower per-unit than POD
- **Cons:** Minimum quantities, upfront investment
- **Cost Estimate:** $12-20/book at 250+ qty

### Traditional Offset (500-1000+ copies)
- **Providers:** Friesens, Hemlock, overseas printers
- **Pros:** Best quality, full customization, lowest per-unit
- **Cons:** Large upfront investment, long lead times
- **Cost Estimate:** $8-15/book at 1000+ qty
